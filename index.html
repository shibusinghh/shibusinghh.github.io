AI & GenAI Strategy

Strategy consulting engagements through the EDHEC Global MBA, 2026.

Situation

Amadeus's Competitive and Market Intelligence leadership needed a structured view of whether AI-native and non-traditional players — agentic assistants, API-first aggregators, travel fintech — could erode its position in travel distribution over a three-to-five-year horizon.

Task

Deliver an eight-week strategy engagement: map the AI disruption landscape, project how it evolves, and recommend a strategic response across build, partner, acquire and monitor tracks.

Action

I structured the disruption hypotheses, designed and ran expert interviews with Amadeus AI, product and distribution leaders, mapped AI-native players by mechanism of disruption rather than sector label, synthesised six disruptor archetypes, and pressure-tested them against scenarios with named leading indicators.

Result

A written strategic study and executive presentation. Core recommendation: Amadeus should not compete at the consumer-assistant layer; its defensible position is the trusted, deterministic execution and offer/order infrastructure layer for AI-mediated travel commerce — with response options mapped to Cytric, Nevio, Outpayce and distribution API access.

Method

Hypothesis-led expert interviews; archetype clustering; scenario development with leading indicators; response-option design (acquire, partner, compete, integrate, monitor, defend). Findings grounded in interview evidence on MCP/UCP protocol evolution, direct-connect traffic and long-tail connectivity cost.

What made it difficult

Separating durable signals from AI hype. The discipline was to classify players by mechanism of disruption and anchor every scenario in evidence from practitioner interviews rather than market commentary.

The bottleneck in agentic travel commerce is not the interface. It is trusted, low-cost connectivity between AI agents and deterministic execution rails.
Situation

LVMH's group-level GenAI customer intelligence platform could create value only if 75 autonomous maisons adopted it — without compromising brand equity, clienteling judgement, data boundaries or maison-level P&L accountability. The constraint was not the technology; it was trust, governance and ways of working.

Task

Define the adoption and operating-model approach: which roles use AI recommendations, which decisions remain human, what governance cross-maison use cases require, and how adoption should be measured.

Action

I segmented maisons and user personas by data maturity and brand-risk sensitivity; mapped human–AI task boundaries across brand, digital, CRM and retail; designed the decision-rights model for cross-maison recommendations; and structured executive future-of-work workshops built around concrete operating-model decisions rather than general AI ambition.

Result

An adoption readiness roadmap with pilot waves and champion networks; human-in-the-loop governance and brand guardrails; and a KPI framework that measures behaviour — recommendation acceptance, override patterns, time-to-insight, repeat usage — rather than platform access alone.

Method

Use-case inventory and prioritisation; maison and persona segmentation; human–AI task mapping; decision-rights and governance design; ADKAR-structured change plan; behavioural adoption analytics.

What made it difficult

Federated autonomy. A group platform can read as central control, so adoption had to be designed opt-in: maison-specific value cases, local governance representation, and explicit rules for where human judgement is non-negotiable.

AI adoption is not a technology rollout. It is a trust, governance and ways-of-working transformation.

Strategy & Sustainability

Business-model and circular-economy strategy, EDHEC Global MBA, 2026.

Situation

Lenovo operated five valid but disconnected circularity programmes — trade-in, asset recovery, certified refurbished, outlet and recycling. The project deck estimated 83% of devices shipped in a year might never return, while the refurbished market was projected to grow from $124B toward $400B over ten years.

Task

Reframe device return from end-of-life logistics to a strategic control point, and propose an integrated operating and commercial model with a testable path to scale.

Action

I contributed to the current-state journey diagnosis, opportunity framing, and the design of one return moment with three pathways — loyalty credit, philanthropy routing, and return-and-refurbish recovery. I built the risk model across strategic, operational, customer, technology/data and external categories, and shaped the MVP: an eight-week UK laptop pilot with one enterprise account, five testable hypotheses and a $250K budget.

Result

A boardroom-grade blueprint: three-year phased roadmap with decision gates; modelled cumulative net impact of $1.65B; projected 3.2× twelve-month MVP return; and a value case covering 89K metric tons of CO₂e avoided annually and 1M+ digital-inclusion beneficiaries on a representative one-million-device cohort.

Method

Customer-journey diagnosis; market sizing; three-pathway operating concept; hypothesis-driven MVP design (credit visibility, data-wipe trust, triage accuracy, ESG certificate value, refurbished sell-through); risk-mitigation architecture including NIST 800-88 data sanitisation, ITAD partner strategy and CSRD audit readiness.

What made it difficult

Avoiding a big-bang recommendation. Pricing ambiguity, cannibalisation, reverse-logistics capacity and data-security trust were genuine unknowns — so the strategy had to earn scale through a narrow, falsifiable pilot rather than assume it.

The return moment is a strategic control point, not a logistics problem.

HR & Technology-Enabled Transformation

Enterprise platform adoption and digital service transformation, EY and Deloitte, 2023–2025.

Situation

WPP, a federation of agency brands across UK, EMEA and Asia, was consolidating fragmented HR practices onto Workday through a £30M+ programme covering 100,000+ employees. The binding constraint was not system replacement — it was trust in standardisation across eight agencies protective of local autonomy.

Task

Execute the change and adoption workstream: translate Workday design decisions into stakeholder impacts, readiness measures and adoption risks, and give the steering committee decision-grade evidence for cutover.

Global HRIS transformation is operating-model and behaviour change. The platform creates the possibility; adoption creates the value.
Situation

A 1,800-employee organisation was implementing a cloud HCM platform across six HR process areas. The common failure mode was in view: the system goes live while users continue relying on HR emails and manual workarounds. The critical risk group was managers, who saw self-service as HR work pushed onto them.

Task

Design and execute the adoption plan — training, risk-prioritised communications and readiness — with adoption measured behaviourally, not by attendance.

Action

I built role-based, task-specific learning paths in place of generic module training; reframed manager communications around faster people decisions rather than added administration; set up floor-walker and super-user support for go-live; and tracked training completion, manager self-service usage and ticket themes through the first twelve weeks.

Result

93% training completion, 78% manager self-service adoption within twelve weeks of go-live, and a 24% reduction in post-go-live support tickets.

Method

Training needs analysis by persona; risk-prioritised communications matrix; ADKAR gap diagnosis; hypercare ticket-theme analysis; readiness dashboard.

What made it difficult

Role acceptance. Managers had to believe the system saved time before they would change behaviour — and HR had to stop quietly accepting requests through old channels, or the new model would never consolidate.

Training completion is an input. Adoption is proven when users perform the new behaviour and support demand falls.

Organisation Design & Operating Model

Structural diagnostics and target operating model design, EY and Deloitte, 2023–2025.

Situation

A post-merger organisation had integrated legally but not operationally: duplicated reporting lines, eight management layers, sub-scale spans around 4:1, unclear decision rights and country-led silos that slowed decisions and diluted accountability.

Task

Build the evidence base for redesign and support the move from country-led silos to a hybrid global-process / local-delivery operating model — without losing critical talent in transition.

Action

I supported the full diagnostic-to-design sequence: organisation diagnostic and duplication analysis; spans-and-layers measurement; activity-based assessment classifying work as globalise, localise, standardise, automate or stop; reporting-line review testing structure against decision rights; and retained-organisation modelling with sequenced role migration and a critical-role retention plan.

Result

Management layers reduced from eight to six, average span of control lifted from 4.1 to 6.7, and approximately 11% managerial overlap removed — implemented through a sequenced migration plan rather than a one-step restructure.

Method

Hierarchy mapping and layer counting; span-of-control analytics; activity-to-role classification; solid/dotted reporting-line audit; decision-rights (RACI) mapping; retained-organisation and transition modelling.

What made it difficult

Structure changes touch status, control and role identity. Country leaders feared loss of local control; the analysis had to separate genuinely local, value-adding nuance from duplicated legacy structure — and the narrative had to frame redesign as enabling strategy, not cutting heads.

Organisation design is not boxes and lines. It is the design of accountability, decision speed and leadership capacity.

Core Change Management & Culture

Operating-model adoption, leadership enablement and workforce transitions, Deloitte and EY, 2020–2025.

Situation

An HR operating model change across 2,400 employees and five business units was stalling on ambiguity: HRBPs pulled into transactional work, managers over-escalating, employees unclear where to go, and readiness measured at 61%.

Task

Lead the change workstream: convert the target operating model from a slide into practical behaviours for each persona, and lift readiness to a defensible pre-go-live level.

Action

I ran stakeholder mapping and change-risk and impact assessments by persona and business unit; established an ADKAR-based readiness baseline; engaged sponsors with specific visibility actions; and delivered targeted interventions — manager enablement sessions, HRBP role-clarity workshops, escalation guidance and business-unit readiness tracking.

Result

Pre-go-live readiness improved from 61% to 84%, and transition escalations fell 28% within ten weeks of go-live.

Method

Persona impact matrix; readiness scorecard across ADKAR dimensions; sponsor action plans; escalation-theme analysis; decision-rights clarification (self-service vs HR operations vs HRBP vs COE).

What made it difficult

The resistance was identity-based: HR practitioners' roles were changing and managers were absorbing new accountability. Each group needed a specific answer to "what do I stop, start and do differently."

People adapt to change faster than they adapt to unclear accountability. Ambiguity, not resistance, is the first enemy.

Succession planning is business-continuity risk management through a people lens — not naming favourites.

Operating Method

A consistent method across every engagement: diagnose with evidence, design for behaviour, govern with data, and quantify the result.

A

Diagnose the real constraint

Stakeholder architecture, change-impact assessment and readiness baselines to locate where the transformation will break — trust, capability, decision rights or data — before design begins.

B

Design for behaviour, not go-live

Role-based training, decision-rights clarity, human-in-the-loop governance, champion networks and manager enablement — so every persona knows what to stop, start and do differently.

C

Govern with evidence

Adoption dashboards, risk registers and readiness gates in Power BI and Tableau. Readiness is measured, reported and defended with data — never with informal confidence.

D

Quantify the outcome

Ticket reduction, self-service uplift, escalation decline, readiness deltas, span and layer movement, quality gains — adoption is tracked as a value stream with numbers leadership can defend.
